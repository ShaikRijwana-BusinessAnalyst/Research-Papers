### 4) 💰 Safeguarding Real Household Savings in India: Fixed Deposit Returns, Inflation Dynamics & Wealth Erosion Risk

**File:** `Working Paper`

#### Relevance
This working paper addresses a critical household finance issue: whether savings in fixed deposits are truly protecting wealth after inflation. It is relevant because many households in India use fixed deposits as a safe savings instrument, but the real value of those savings depends on inflation-adjusted returns. In periods of elevated inflation, nominal returns may look positive while real purchasing power quietly declines.

#### Key Findings
The paper examines the gap between fixed deposit returns, inflation trends, and real household wealth preservation. The central insight is that nominal safety does not automatically mean real safety. If inflation outpaces deposit returns, households may experience wealth erosion even when their savings appear secure. The analysis shows that real return assessment is essential when evaluating household financial security.

#### Impact
This paper has direct implications for households, financial advisors, banks, and policymakers. It helps explain why savers need to look beyond nominal interest rates and consider inflation risk, liquidity, and portfolio diversification. At a broader level, it supports financial literacy and strengthens understanding of how inflation can silently weaken household wealth over time.
